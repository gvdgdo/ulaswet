# Unlocking Legal Automation with Semantic Web Technology

## Guido Governatori (Central Queensland Univesity) and Monica Palmirani (University of Bologna)

### Motivation and relevance

The increasing complexity of legal systems and the growing demand for
efficient compliance solutions have highlighted the need for advanced
legal automation technologies. Despite the growth of Artificial
Intelligence and Machine learning, these technologies often struggle to
capture the nuanced and context-dependent nature of legal norms. Legal
automation requires not only the ability to represent legal texts in
structured formats but also to capture their normative meaning in a way
that supports automated reasoning, and it requires a deep understanding
of the legal domain and the ability to model legal concepts in a way
that is both accurate, computationally tractable, transparent and
explainable.

Semantic web standards like Akoma Ntoso and LegalRuleML provide powerful
tools for structuring legal knowledge and modelling legal norms,
enabling the development of automated systems that can interpret and
reason about legal texts. This tutorial is highly relevant for
researchers and practitioners in the fields of legal informatics,
artificial intelligence, and knowledge representation, as it offers
practical insights into how these standards can be applied to real-world
legal automation challenges.

### Content, format and program

The tutorial will be structured into several sections, each focusing on
a specific aspect of legal automation using semantic web technologies.
The content will include both theoretical concepts and practical
applications, with hands-on demonstrations to illustrate key points.

Specifically, the tutorial will cover the following topics:

#### Introduction to Legal Automation

- Challenges in interpreting and operationalising legal texts

- From legal documents to executable compliance systems

- Overview of standards for legal knowledge representation

#### Akoma Ntoso: Structuring Legal Documents

- Principles of Akoma Ntoso (XML-based legal document standard)

- Markup of legislative and regulatory texts

- Semantic annotation of legal elements (articles, sections, references)

- Linking legal texts across documents and jurisdictions

- Annotating legal elements for temporal elements

#### From Text to Norms

- Extracting norms from structured legal documents

- Identifying obligations, permissions, and prohibitions

- Role of metadata and document structure in legal interpretation

#### LegalRuleML: Modelling Legal Norms

- Overview of LegalRuleML and its foundations

- Representation of deontic concepts (obligation, prohibition,
  permission)

- Modelling exceptions, priorities, and conflicts

- Capturing temporal and contextual aspects of norms

#### Linking Akoma Ntoso and LegalRuleML

- Transforming Akoma Ntoso documents into LegalRuleML rules

- Anchoring rules to legal text provisions

- Traceability between legal sources and executable rules

#### Rule-Based Reasoning and Compliance Checking

- Execution of LegalRuleML rules using rule engines

- Defeasible reasoning and conflict resolution

- Automated compliance verification workflows

#### Explainability and Legal Traceability

- Generating explanations grounded in legal sources

- Justifying decisions through linked provisions

- Supporting auditability and accountability

#### Hands-on Demonstration

- Annotating a legal text in Akoma Ntoso

- Encoding norms in LegalRuleML

- Running a simple compliance check

### Level

The tutorial is designed for an audience with a basic understanding of
semantic web technologies and legal informatics. It will cover both
theoretical concepts and practical applications, making it suitable for
both researchers and practitioners in the field of legal automation.

### Target Audience

This tutorial is intended for researchers and practitioners interested
in the application of semantic web technologies to legal automation. It
is suitable for those with a background in computer science, artificial
intelligence, and legal informatics, as well as legal professionals
interested in the intersection of law and technology.

### Prerequisites

Participants are expected to have a basic understanding of semantic web
technologies (e.g., XML, RDF). Familiarity with legal document
structures and normative concepts (e.g., obligations, permissions) will
be beneficial but is not required.

Knowledge and Information Systems (JURIX) conference, International
Summer School of Reasoning Web, the Artificial Intelligence and Law

### Monica Palmirani

LegalDocML and LegalRuleML and has served on the OASIS LegalXML Steering
Committee since 2013. In 2015, she was recognised as an OASIS
Distinguished Contributor and served on the OASIS Board of Directors

more than 130 papers, chaired international conferences, edited book

Journal. She has been President of IAAIL (2024--2025), vice-coordinator
of the international PhD programme "Law, Science and Technologies"
